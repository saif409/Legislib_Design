Judgement


1. This is an application under section 42(2) read with section 43 of the Arbitration Act 2001, filed by the respondent-petitioner, Jalalabad Gas Transmission and Distribution System Limited, against the claimant respondent, Lafarge Surma Cement Limited, against the Umpire Tribunal, consisting of Mr. Justice Md. Tafazzal Islam as Chairman/Umpire, Dr Gavan Griffith QC and Mr. Justice Md. Fazlul Karim the former honorable Chief Justice of Bangladesh as Arbitrators for the contesting parties. In order to resolve the dispute, arisen as to the payment of gas bill by the Lafarge Surma Cement Limited, the 1st party claimant, to the Jalalabad Gas Distribution and System Limited, the 2nd party. 2. The aforesaid Arbitration Tribunal was constituted by the parties Lafarge Surma Cement Limited as 1st party-claimant and the Jalalabad Gas Distribution and System Limited as 2nd party in order resolve the dispute arisen in between them in respect of payment of gas bill, so far the buyers force majeure invoked by the claimant for the period of 28-10-2007 up to 29-11-2007 and also for the period 12-4-2010 up to 15-5-2010. The said arbitration was held in Dhaka and two of the arbitrators being Dr. Gavan Griffith QC and Mr. Md. Justice Fazlul Karim have issued split award for which the umpire his lordship Mr. Justice Tafazzal Islam has also issued separate award, which, being final, resolved the issue in between the parties in the following manner; In respect of the claim of claimant against the buyer force majeure for the period between 12 April 2010 and 9 August 2011, the tribunal declares that: (a) Except for the period between 12 April 2010 and 15 May 2010, the invocation of buyer force majeure by the Claimant is valid. (b) The obligation of the claimant during the buyer force majeure was limited to payment of gas bills for gas actually consumed. (c) The claimant must pay the respondent the moneys due for Take-or-Pay Quantity of gas during the period between 12 April 2010 and 15 May 2010 together with simple interest calculated at the rate of 6% from the due date for payment until paid under this award. (d) The claimant is not otherwise liable to pay further moneys to the respondent under the terms of the GSA. (e) The claimant is awarded costs of Tk. 75,00,000 (Taka Seventy five lacs only). 3. Being aggrieved with and highly dissatisfied by the said umpire award, the 2nd party respondent Jalalabad Gas Distribution and System Limited preferred the instant application under section 42(2) of the Arbitration Act, 2001 as petitioner on certain grounds mentioned in the application itself and prayed for cancellation of the impugned award issued by the umpire and to approve the split award passed by his lordship Mr. Justice Md. Fazlul Karim. 4. The 1st party-claimant, herein respondent Lafarge Surma Cement Limited appeared and filed affidavit-in-opposition, dated 31-3-2016 entry No. 1725, wherein it has been asserted that the grounds taken by the petitioner-2nd party in the instant application are merely repeat of the contention and submission made by the 2nd party in the arbitration regarding the substance of the dispute and since the arbitral tribunal having considered these contentions and submissions it do not make out any ground whatsoever for getting a setting aside of the arbitration award, under the provision of section 42 of the Arbitration Act, 2001. The findings of the arbitral tribunal on the substance of the dispute being valid and sound under the applicable law, it does not warrant any interference by this court, under the provision of section 42 of the Arbitration Act, 2001. 5.
